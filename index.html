Newton's Laws of Motion and Friction Cheat Sheet

1. Newton's Laws of Motion

Law Statement Formula
1st Law (Law of Inertia) An object remains in its state of rest or uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force. N/A
2nd Law (Force and Acceleration) The net force acting on an object is equal to the product of its mass and acceleration. F = ma
3rd Law (Action and Reaction) For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. \(F_{\text{action}} = -F_{\text{reaction}}\)

2. Forces in Motion

Force Formula Description
Gravitational Force F = mg Force due to gravity, where \(m\) is mass and \(g\) is gravitational acceleration (\(9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2\))
Normal Force N = mg cosθ Perpendicular force exerted by a surface on an object
Tension T = mg + ma Force exerted by a string or rope on an object
Net Force F_net = ΣF Sum of all forces acting on an object
Centripetal Force F_c = mv²/r Force acting towards the center in circular motion

3. Friction

Type Formula Description
Static Friction f_s ≤ μ_s N Friction that prevents motion, up to a maximum limit
Kinetic Friction f_k = μ_k N Friction acting on a moving object
Rolling Friction f_r = μ_r N Friction acting on a rolling object

4. Inclined Plane with Friction

Force Formula Description
Parallel Component of Weight F_parallel = mg sinθ Component of gravity along the plane
Perpendicular Component of Weight F_perpendicular = mg cosθ Component of gravity perpendicular to the plane
Frictional Force f = μ N Frictional force opposing motion, where \(N = F_{\text{perpendicular}}\)
Net Force F_net = F_parallel - f Net force causing motion down the incline

5. Work-Energy Relation for Friction

Quantity Formula Description
Work Done by Friction W_f = f × d × cosθ Work done by friction force over distance \(d\)
Power P = W/t Rate of work done
