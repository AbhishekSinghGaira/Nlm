Newton's Laws of Motion and Friction Cheat Sheet

1. Newton's Laws of Motion

Law Statement Formula
1st Law (Law of Inertia) An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force. Not Applicable
2nd Law (Force and Acceleration) The net force acting on an object is the product of its mass and acceleration. F = ma
3rd Law (Action and Reaction) For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. \( F = -F \)

2. Forces in Motion

Force Formula Description
Gravitational Force \( F = mg \) Force due to gravity where \( g = 9.8 \, m/s^2 \).
Normal Force \( N = mg \cos \theta \) Perpendicular force exerted by a surface.
Tension \( T = mg + ma \) Force exerted by a string or rope.
Net Force \( F_{net} = \Sigma F \) Sum of all forces acting on an object.
Centripetal Force \( F_c = \frac{mv^2}{r} \) Force directed towards the center in circular motion.

3. Friction

Type Formula Description
Static Friction \( f_s \leq \mu_s N \) Friction that prevents motion up to a maximum limit.
Kinetic Friction \( f_k = \mu_k N \) Friction acting on a moving object.
Rolling Friction \( f_r = \mu_r N \) Friction acting on a rolling object.

4. Inclined Plane with Friction

Force Formula Description
Parallel Component of Weight \( F_{parallel} = mg \sin \theta \) Component of gravity along the plane.
Perpendicular Component of Weight \( F_{perpendicular} = mg \cos \theta \) Component of gravity perpendicular to the plane.
Frictional Force \( f = \mu N \) Frictional force opposing motion.
Net Force \( F_{net} = F_{parallel} - f \) Net force causing motion down the incline.

5. Work-Energy Relation for Friction

Quantity Formula Description
Work Done by Friction \( W_f = f \cdot d \cdot \cos \theta \) Work done by friction force over a distance \( d \).
Power \( P = \frac{W}{t} \) Rate of work done.
